RULES

Introduction

Welcome to RC Car Race(electric only), a thrilling enthusiast competition to check the control of players on their joysticks.

Registration start: 21st February

Registration Deadline: 2nd March

Event Day: 5th March

Event Flow

  • 10:00 AM: The registrations will be verified on 5th March at NST X ADYPU.
  • 11:00-3:00 PM: Rounds
  • 3:00 - 4:00 PM: Finals
  • 4:30: Winners announced

Race Format

  • RC cars will compete against each other in designated race rounds.
  • The competition format and round details will be communicated to teams one day before the event.

Technical Specifications

  • A portfolio submission is required, including car details and a working model video.

Dimensional Specifications

  • Length: Max 55 cm (end-to-end of front and rear tires)
  • Width: Max 30 cm
  • Height: Max 30 cm (with fully compressed suspension)
  • Motors: Sensor-based or sensorless allowed (all-electric classes)

Radio Equipment

  • Channels: Only two (for steering and throttle servos)
  • Frequency: 2.4 GHz DSM/DSS or any allowed (must notify transmission details)
  • Restrictions:
    • No advanced radio features beyond control/trimming
    • No frequency swapping via transmitter/receiver crystals
    • No traction control systems

Tyres

  • Cutting tyres to fit different wheels is prohibited

Battery

  • Max Voltage: 24V (across any two points)

Transmission & Drive

  • Allowed: Single-speed transmissions with 2WD or 4WD

Arena Specifications

  • 1. Track Length: 50– 100 meters (Longer for high-speed sections)
  • 2. Track Width: 2-4 meters to allow overtaking and maneuvering.
  • 3. Surface: Mixed terrain (e.g., dirt, asphalt, or carpet) to challenge drivers' adaptability.
  • 5. Pit Area: A designated area for repairs or battery swaps.
  • 6. Safety: Barriers or foam padding around the track to protect both cars and spectators.
  • 9. Lap Timing System:Stopwatch-based (Simple for casual races).
  • 10. Spectator Area: Designated viewing areas to keep the audience safe and engaged.

On-Track Rules

Obstacle Navigation:

  • Teams must complete all obstacles in the correct order.

Crashes and Stops:

  • If a car flips or crashes, the team may reset the car to the last completed obstacle and continue.
  • The clock will not stop during resets.

Track Boundaries:

  • Cars must stay within the designated track boundaries.

Marshaling

Marshals:

  • Marshals will be stationed around the track to assist with flipped cars and enforce rules.
  • Teams may be required to marshal during other teams' runs.

Marshal Etiquette:

  • Marshals must not interfere with cars unless necessary.
  • Cars should be placed back on the track at the last completed obstacle.

Spectator Rules

Spectator Area:

  • Spectators must remain in designated areas and not enter the track or pit area.
  • Children must be supervised at all times.

Interference:

  • Spectators must not interfere with the race or distract drivers.

Disputes and Appeals

Race Director:

  • The race director has the final say in all disputes and rule interpretations.

Appeals:

  • Teams may appeal a decision by submitting a written complaint to the organizers after the race.

Miscellaneous

Weather:

  • In case of bad weather (for outdoor events), the race may be postponed or canceled.

Equipment Failure:

  • Organizers are not responsible for equipment failure or damage to RC cars during the event.

Liability:

  • Participants and spectators attend the event at their own risk. Organizers are not liable for any injuries or damages.
